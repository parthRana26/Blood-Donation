About Us

Blood donation is a voluntary medical procedure where a person allows their blood to be drawn for use in transfusions or to be made into biopharmaceutical medications through a process known as fractionation1. This process can involve donating whole blood or specific components of the blood, such as red cells, plasma, or platelets.

About Blood Doners

Most donors give blood as an act of charity or community service, and in many places, blood donors are unpaid volunteers. However, some countries allow paid donations or offer incentives like paid time off from work1. The frequency with which a person can donate blood varies depending on the type of donation and the regulations of the country where the donation takes place1.

Donation Process

The donation process from the time you arrive center until the time you leave

1 - Registration

We’ll sign you in and go over basic eligibility.You’ll be asked to show ID, such as your driver’s license.You’ll read some information about donating blood.We’ll ask you for your complete address. Your address needs to be completeand the place where you will receive your mail 8 weeks from donation.

2 - Health screening

We make sure it is safe for you to donate, and that your blood donation is safe for a patient to receive.

3 - Donation

the donation time can vary from 8-10 minutes for whole blood13 to 80-120 minutes for platelets, red cells, or plasma4.

4 - Save Life

Your donation is transported to one of our blood centres where it is tested and processed before being issued to hospitals. If you’ve ever wondered what happens to your donation, we've created a video that follows your blood from when it leaves your arm, right the way through to being used in a hospital.
